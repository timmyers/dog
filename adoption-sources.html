Adoption Tips

  1. Assess your lifestyle honestly. Herding breeds need 1–2 hours of daily exercise plus mental stimulation. Goldens need 2+ hours. Make sure the idea of constantly challenging your dog excites you.
  2. Ask for detailed behavioral info. Foster-based rescues know their dogs well — ask about behavior on walks, with other dogs, when left alone, and in a home environment.
  3. Follow the 3-3-3 rule. Rescue dogs need about 3 days to decompress, 3 weeks to settle in, and 3 months to truly feel safe and bonded. Be patient.
  4. Schedule a vet visit in the first week. Even with rescue-provided health records, get a fresh checkup. Ask about MDR1 gene testing for herding breeds.
  5. Dog-proof your home before arrival. Remove toxic plants (aloe vera, ivy, lilies), secure trash, and set up a quiet retreat space.
  6. Consider a dog with a known history. Foster-based rescues (vs. shelters) can tell you much more about a dog's real-world behavior.
  7. Be ready for herding breed quirks. They may nip at heels, try to herd children or other pets, and can be vocal. Manageable with training, but know what you're signing up for.
