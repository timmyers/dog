First Days with Ilia

  1. Follow the 3-3-3 rule. Ilia needs about 3 days to decompress, 3 weeks to settle in, and 3 months to truly feel safe and bonded. Be patient — she may be shy or anxious at first.
  2. Schedule a vet visit this week. Get a fresh checkup even with RezDawg's health records. Ask about MDR1 gene testing since she's a herding breed mix.
  3. Set up her safe space. A crate in the bedroom with a blanket, plus a quiet retreat area she can go to when overwhelmed. Let her explore the house gradually.
  4. Keep it calm. Resist the urge to introduce her to everyone right away. Let her bond with you first before adding new people, dogs, and environments.
  5. Start a routine immediately. Regular feeding times, potty breaks every 2-3 hours (she's a puppy), and consistent sleep schedule. Predictability builds trust.
  6. Expect some accidents. New environment resets housetraining. Enzymatic cleaner, no punishment, reward heavily for going outside.
  7. Watch for herding breed quirks. She may nip at heels, try to herd, and be vocal. Redirect with toys and positive reinforcement — don't punish.
